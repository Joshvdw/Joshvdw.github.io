What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?


In this blog I'll be explaining (in plain English) what the difference is between these CSS attributes.

All of these attributes relate to the "box-model", which is essentially an invisible box that wraps around every element on a webpage.

Manipulating this box-model is how a developer structures & styles the layout of content on the site.

Now to break down the difference between margin, border & padding, let's first start with margin - which is the outermost attribute of the box-model.

Margin measures the space on the outside of the box-model.

Border is the outline or stroke of the box-model.

Padding measure the space on the inside of the box-model.

Think of it like you're putting a sealed-container of left-over food into the fridge. Margin is the amount of space you leave around the container, so that it isn't touching other food items in the fridge. Border is the plastic thickness that makes up the food-container and padding is the space between the actual food and the inside edges of the container. The food is the content on the webpage in this instance.
